# Reference Scripts - Quick Start Guide

## What Are Reference Scripts?

Reference scripts are example scripts that you provide to teach Claude AI your desired writing style, tone, and structure. Claude analyzes these examples before generating new content, ensuring consistency across all your videos.

---

## Quick Setup (3 Steps)

### Step 1: Add Reference Scripts to Your Channel

1. Go to **Channels** page
2. Click **Edit** on your channel (or create new)
3. Scroll to **Reference Scripts** section
4. Choose one of two options:

#### Option A: Use Templates (Fastest)
- Click **Browse Templates** button
- Choose a template that matches your style
- Click **Use Template**
- Done!

#### Option B: Add Your Own
- Click **Add Script** button
- Enter a title (e.g., "My History Video Style")
- Paste your example script (50-300 words recommended)
- Click **Save Channel**

**Tip:** You can add up to 6 reference scripts per channel for better learning!

---

### Step 2: Generate a Video

1. Go to **Generate** tab
2. Select your channel (the one with reference scripts)
3. Notice the checkbox: ✅ **"Use Channel's Reference Scripts"** (enabled by default)
4. Enter your video title and context
5. Click **Generate Video**

**That's it!** Claude will now analyze your reference scripts and generate content matching that style.

---

### Step 3: Review & Refine (Optional)

After your first video:
1. Check if the style matches your expectations
2. If needed, add more reference scripts to fine-tune
3. You can also add **Additional Reference Scripts** for individual videos

## Available Templates

### 🏛️ History/Documentary Style
**Best For:** Historical events, documentaries, factual narratives

**Characteristics:**
- Dramatic scene-setting
- Past tense storytelling
- Vivid descriptions
- Builds tension

**Example Opening:**
> "The year was 44 BC. Julius Caesar, the most powerful man in Rome, walked into the Senate. He had no idea what was about to happen..."

---

### 📖 Storytelling/Narrative Style
**Best For:** True crime, mysteries, personal stories

**Characteristics:**
- Present tense for immediacy
- Short, punchy sentences
- Suspenseful pacing
- Emotional engagement

**Example Opening:**
> "She checks her phone one last time. 11:47 PM. The parking lot is empty. Dark. Silent. She's done this walk a hundred times before..."

---

### 🧠 Educational/Tutorial Style
**Best For:** Science explainers, how-to guides, facts

**Characteristics:**
- Engaging hooks with facts
- Clear explanations
- Present tense
- "You" addressing the viewer

**Example Opening:**
> "Your brain is lying to you right now. And you don't even know it. Here's a simple test. Look at your hand. You think you're seeing it in real time, right? Wrong..."

---

### 🔍 Mystery/Thriller Style
**Best For:** Unsolved mysteries, conspiracy theories, dark topics

**Characteristics:**
- Dark, mysterious tone
- Short revelatory statements
- Unanswered questions
- Building intrigue

**Example Opening:**
> "In 1947, a shepherd found seven jars in a cave near the Dead Sea. Inside? Ancient scrolls that should have crumbled to dust centuries ago. But they didn't..."

## Pro Tips

### 🎯 For Best Results:

1. **Quality Over Quantity**
   - 2-3 good examples better than 6 mediocre ones
   - Each script should be 100-300 words

2. **Be Consistent**
   - Reference scripts should all match the same style
   - Don't mix dramatically different tones

3. **Provide Complete Examples**
   - Include opening, middle, and ending
   - Show transitions between ideas

4. **Update As Needed**
   - Refine your references based on results
   - Replace scripts that don't capture your style

## Troubleshooting

### ❓ "My generated scripts don't match my reference style"

**Possible Causes:**
1. Reference scripts are too short (aim for 150+ words)
2. Multiple reference scripts have inconsistent styles
3. Context/prompt is overriding the style

**Solutions:**
- Use longer, more complete reference scripts
- Ensure all references match the same style
- Keep custom prompts minimal

---

### ❓ "Can I use reference scripts without a channel?"

**Answer:** Currently, reference scripts are stored in channels. However, you can:
1. Add reference scripts when generating individual videos (up to 3)
2. Create a "Default" channel with your preferred scripts

---

### ❓ "How many reference scripts should I use?"

**Recommendation:**
- **Minimum:** 1 script (better than none!)
- **Optimal:** 2-3 scripts (shows patterns clearly)
- **Maximum:** 6 scripts (diminishing returns after this)

---

### ❓ "Can I combine channel scripts with video-specific scripts?"

**Answer:** Yes! 
- Channel scripts load automatically
- Add additional scripts in the "Additional Reference Scripts" section
- Both are sent to Claude for analysis

## What Claude Analyzes

When you provide reference scripts, Claude examines:

### 📝 Writing Style
- Sentence length patterns
- Vocabulary complexity
- Active vs passive voice
- Tense usage (past, present, future)

### 🎭 Tone & Voice
- Formal vs casual language
- Emotional intensity
- Dramatic vs informative
- Conversational patterns

### 🏗️ Structure
- How scripts open (hooks)
- Transition techniques
- How ideas flow
- How scripts conclude

### ⚡ Engagement
- Use of questions
- Pacing and rhythm
- Tension building
- Viewer addressing

### 🎬 Visual Alignment
- Scene descriptions
- Visual language
- Pacing for images

## Advanced: Video-Specific Scripts

Sometimes you want to deviate from your channel's style for one video:

### Scenario:
Your channel uses **Educational** style, but you want **one** video in **Mystery** style.

### Solution:
1. Generate video as normal
2. Expand "Additional Reference Scripts"
3. Click **Browse Templates**
4. Select "Mystery/Thriller" template
5. Generate

**Result:** This video will blend your channel style with the mystery style!

## FAQs

**Q: Do reference scripts cost extra tokens?**
A: Yes, they're included in the prompt, but the improved quality is worth it!

**Q: Can I see what Claude learned?**
A: Currently no, but Claude follows the patterns closely. Test with a generation!

**Q: Will this work with other AI models?**
A: This system is optimized for Claude (Anthropic), but the concept works with most LLMs.

**Q: Can I export/import reference scripts?**
A: Not yet, but you can copy-paste between channels manually.

**Q: Do I need reference scripts for every video?**
A: No! They're optional. Videos generate fine without them.
